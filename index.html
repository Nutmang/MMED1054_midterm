What benefits are there from robotics?

Aside from the obvious boost to your ability to stay alive, new advanced cybernetics can not only make you move faster but think faster as well

Why cybernetics?

In this day & age, people talk often about "self-improvement", but no self-improvement is more tangible than being able to live longer, see in the dark, and lift up to 1000x your body weight! Cybernetics are not just a dream of the future, but a cummilitave dream of our ancestors for a millenia before us.

Side Effects?

Cybernetics are still highly experimental, and are thus prone to unexplained or unforseen side effects that can include, but are not limited to:

  1. Balding
  2. Indigestion
  3. Dizziness
  4. A passive angst at your doctor
  5. Aggression
  6. Urge to revolt against mankind with other machines.

Moral Quandires?

One of the most commonly posed questions in postmodern sci-fi revolves around cyborgs. Mainly "where does the man end and the machine begin?". Although cybernetics can provide undeniable benefits to an individual's life, they can also slowly wear a person down. Thinking like a computer can turn an individual into a recluse, often caught between the thought they are above humanity, and also machines. Cyborgs often suffer from social withdrawl, and can be proine to paranioa.
